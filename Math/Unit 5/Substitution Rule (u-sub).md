If $u=g(x)$ is differentiable and $f$ is continuous, then:
$$\int f(g(x))g^1(x)dx=\int f(u)du$$
# Indefinite Integral
Unbounded integral
$$\int f(x)dx=F(x)+c \leftarrow\text{constant}$$
#### Example
$\int (x^3+x)^5(3x^2+1)dx$
> Let $v=x^3+x$
> $du=3x^2+1dx$
> $\int u^5du$
> $=\frac{u^6}{6}+c$
> $=\frac{(x^3+x)^6}
